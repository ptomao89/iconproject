Status of Basement Apartments in NYC

As New York faces a housing crisis, much of the remaining affordable rental stock available to working class or immigrant populations are found in basement dwellings. 53% of New Yorkers pay more that 30% of their income to housing.

Roughly 50,000-100,000 New Yorkers live in unlicensed basement apartments. Many of these units lack windows and safety measures required by New York City building code. An estimated 30,000 unlicensed basement units are vulnerable to flooding in New York.

Under current regulatory measures, it could cost roughly $300,000 to convert a basement unit into legal housing. A financial barrier that many small landlords cannot overcome.

New legislation would provide financing for landlords looking to upgrade or convert basement units into housing. Additionally, it removes burdensome regulations and extends legal protections to renters living in basements. Rent would be stabilized for current basement renters.
